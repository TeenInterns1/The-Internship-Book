Not all internships are created equal! There are many different types of internship experiences available to high school students, each with their own unique benefits and challenges. This visual guide expands on Chapter 2 of our book with detailed illustrations of the different types of internships you might encounter.

Click on any illustration to view it in detail. Each image includes key information about that type of internship, including typical responsibilities, benefits, challenges, and industries where they're common.

Corporate Internship

Structured programs at large companies that often offer mentorship and competitive perks.

Nonprofit Internship

Mission-driven opportunities that combine hands-on work with community impact.

Startup Internship

Dynamic, fast-paced roles where you'll gain diverse experience and significant responsibility.

Research Internship

Academic-focused opportunities at universities or laboratories to explore specific fields.

Remote Internship

Virtual positions that offer flexibility and the ability to work from anywhere.

Government Internship

Public service roles that help you understand policy-making and civic leadership.

Creative Internship

Opportunities in design, media, writing, and other artistic fields to build your portfolio.

International Internship

Global experiences that combine professional development with cultural immersion.

Self-Created Internship

Custom-designed experiences where you propose and develop your own role.

How to Choose the Right Type of Internship for You

When deciding which type of internship to pursue, consider the following factors:

  • Your Career Goals: If you have a specific career path in mind, look for internships that align with that industry or role.
  • Your Learning Style: Do you thrive in structured environments, or do you prefer more flexibility and independence?
  • Your Values: If making a social impact is important to you, nonprofit or government internships might be more fulfilling.
  • Your Schedule: Consider your other commitments and whether you need a flexible or remote option.
  • Your Skill Development Needs: Think about what skills you want to develop and which internship type will best help you build those.

Remember that there's no "best" type of internship‚Äîonly the best fit for your unique situation and goals!
